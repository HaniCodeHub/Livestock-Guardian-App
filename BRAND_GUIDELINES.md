## Writing Style Guide

### Tone of Voice
- **Professional but friendly**: Not corporate, not too casual
- **Clear and direct**: Avoid jargon
- **Helpful and supportive**: We're here to help
- **Honest and transparent**: Admit limitations

### Writing Guidelines

**Do**:
- Use active voice: "Register your animal" not "Your animal can be registered"
- Use second person: "You can track your herd" not "Users can track their herds"
- Be specific: "Identify your animal in 3 seconds" not "Fast identification"
- Use simple words: "start" not "initiate", "help" not "facilitate"
- Use inclusive language: Avoid gender/cultural assumptions

**Don't**:
- Use jargon unnecessarily
- Use exclamation marks excessively
- Use ALL CAPS (except acronyms)
- Use double negatives: "Don't forget to register" not "Don't not register"

### Terminology

**Preferred Terms**:
- "Register" (not "create", "add", "enroll")
- "Identify" (not "match", "recognize")
- "Livestock" (not "cattle only", be inclusive)
- "Ownership verification" (not "proof of ownership")
- "Biometric" (not "fingerprint")

## Communication Standards

### Email Communication

**Subject Line Format**:
```
[STATUS] Topic: Brief description

Example:
[ALERT] Livestock Guardian: Important Security Update
[FEATURE] New Offline Mode Now Available
```

**Email Structure**:
1. Greeting (personalized if possible)
2. Main message (why they're receiving this)
3. Call to action (what to do)
4. Support information (how to get help)
5. Sign-off

### Social Media

**Platform Tones**:
- **Twitter**: Quick updates, conversational
- **LinkedIn**: Professional insights, case studies
- **Instagram**: Visual stories, behind-the-scenes
- **Facebook**: Community engagement, announcements

**Posting Guidelines**:
- Frequency: 3-5 posts per week
- Engagement: Respond within 24 hours
- Hashtags: Use 3-5 relevant hashtags
- Images: Always include visuals
- Links: Direct to most relevant resource

## Visual Standards

### Image Guidelines

- Diverse representation of farmers and livestock
- Natural lighting, avoid overly edited
- Authentic moments, not staged
- High resolution (minimum 1200x800px)

- Use consistent icon style (Material Design icons)
- Maintain consistent sizing
- Use brand colors

- Keep data-to-ink ratio high
- Use simple, clear language

- Include data sources

## Trademark Usage

### Proper Usage
- "Livestock Guardian™" (with trademark symbol on first use)
- "Livestock Guardian's identification system"
- Don't use as verb: "Livestock Guardian-ing" ❌

### Logo Requirements
- Minimum size: 128x128 pixels
- Clear space: Maintain space around logo
- No color changes unless specified
- Don't rotate or distort
- Use official files only

## Brand Partnerships

### Co-branding Guidelines
- Partner logo should not exceed 50% of Livestock Guardian logo size
- Equal spacing maintained between logos
- Approval required for joint use
- Usage must be reciprocal
